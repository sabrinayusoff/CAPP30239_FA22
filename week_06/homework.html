Number of People Shot by Police, by Race

This bar chart shows the breakdown of race amongst the people shot by police in 2015. I picked this chart type as it is easy to view the differences in count across race group. I also included the percentage of each race group out of the total number of people shot by police. However, this is not a complete depiction of reality. It would be more accurate to show the percentage of people shot out of the total population in each race group. Using population statistics would reflect this better. Additionally, there is a sizeable proportion of people (7.42%) whose race is unknown.

Proportion with Mental Illness

This donut chart shows the proportion of people shot by the police who had mental illness. I picked this chart because there are only two categories to be represented, and so there would not be difficulty viewing the labels of each category. It might be more helpful to know if signs of mental illness were visible or observed by the police officers who shot the persons with mental illness, to dig deeper into understanding better the thought process of these officers when deciding to shoot such persons.

Proportion of People Armed By State

This stacked horizontal bar chart shows the number of people who were armed, organized by state. I picked this chart as it not only shows the breakdown of people who were armed in each state, but also allows us to compare the overall number of people shot in each state. To simplify the data, I assumed that any person who was not labelled as "unarmed" or with no label, was armed. This may not have been the best decision as it is not clear why there are empty strings for a sizeable number of cases. Additionally, I did not break down the types of weapons in possession of those who were armed, and these vary greatly, including: gun, chain, blunt object and screwdriver. We see that California had by far the highest overall number of people shot by the police. A more accurate visualization would be to show the per capita number of people shot, as the population of California is more than 30% larger than that of Texas, so they are not comparable. Nevertheless, another observation we can make from the chart is that in most states, more armed persons were shot by police than unarmed persons. This could be clearer had I included tooltips or labels to more clearly view the proportion of armed persons in each state.
